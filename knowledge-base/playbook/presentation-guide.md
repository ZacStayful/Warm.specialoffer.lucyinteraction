# Stayful Presentation Delivery Guide

How to present the Stayful offer across different lead types.
Built from 70-meeting intelligence on what worked and what did not.

---

## Presentation Sequence

The standard sequence. Adapt order by archetype.

1. Understand first (discovery)
2. Show the income comparison (the number)
3. Explain the model (how we work)
4. Address protection (damage, guests, exit)
5. Handle objections
6. Agree on next step

**Never start with the pitch.** Discovery always comes first. The income number lands harder when you know their baseline.

---

## The Income Number

The STR net figure is the most important single moment in the meeting.

**How to land it**:
1. Ask their current LTL income or mortgage first
2. Show the STR gross figure
3. Subtract fees visibly: "15% management is £X. Platform fee is another £Y. Cleaning is covered. Your net is £Z."
4. State the gap: "That's £[gap] more per month than your current £[baseline]. Per year that's £[annual gap]."
5. Pause. Let it land. Don't immediately start talking.

**Common mistakes**:
- Showing gross without showing the fee deductions — looks like hiding something
- Not knowing their baseline before showing the figure
- Rushing past the number to get to the model explanation

## Adapting by Archetype

### certainty-seeker
- Lead with protection, not income
- Open with: "Before we look at numbers, what are your main concerns about STR?"
- Address concerns first, then show income
- Leave time for their questions — do not rush
- Send the management agreement during or immediately after the meeting

### loss-averse-switcher
- Lead with the income comparison: LTL vs STR net side by side
- Show seasonal floor (January worst case) explicitly before they ask
- Emphasise: "Even in the worst month, you're at X. That compares to your current Y."
- Reversibility is important: "You can always go back to LTL if it doesn't work. The 6-month term isn't a trap."

### analytical-evaluator
- Have the STR net analyser pulled up and visible
- Go slowly through fee structure — they will notice if you rush
- Answer their questions before continuing the pitch
- Offer to send the full breakdown in writing
- Say "I'll confirm that figure" rather than guessing when you don't know

### urgency-driven
- Move fast. They came ready.
- Confirm their timeline early: "When do you want to be live?"
- Give a specific go-live date during the meeting
- Offer to book photography before the meeting ends
- Send the management agreement within the hour

### betrayal-damaged
- Let them tell the full story of what went wrong first
- Do not interrupt or minimise
- Address their specific failure point before showing any income
- Longer meetings. Multiple touch points. Trust-building takes time.
- Never claim superiority over their previous agent directly — let the specifics of Stayful's process speak

### status-quo-resistant
- Shorter presentation. Don't oversell.
- The income gap is the entire case. Show it clearly.
- Leave them with the number and don't push
- Quarterly check-ins are appropriate. They will come when their situation changes.

### fast-path-gain-focused
- Lead with income. This is what they came for.
- Show the maximum income achievable for their property
- Address the 15% fee head-on — show it as a small slice of a larger income
- Watch for R2R signals — if present, verify landlord consent before continuing

### social-proof-dependent
- Bring third-party evidence early: portfolio size, Airbnb Superhost status, specific comparable property examples
- Offer to connect with an existing owner who is willing to speak
- Do not rely solely on Zac's own claims — they need validation from others

## The Fee Conversation

**Do not open with a fee negotiation.** 15% is the standard rate.

If they raise the fee as a concern:
1. Acknowledge it: "Fair question."
2. Reframe it: "Let's look at what you keep after the fee, not the fee itself."
3. Show the net: "After our 15% and platform costs, your net is £X. That's £Y more than your current income."
4. The fee looks small when the gain is large.

If they push after the reframe:
- For EX-STL leads who are genuinely fee-blocked: 13% is available as a close tool
- 12% requires explicit Zac approval and is not to be offered otherwise

## London Property Protocol

For any lead with a London postcode (or property in Greater London):

1. Confirm the postcode
2. Flag the 90-day annual STR limit under planning rules
3. Ask if they have verified their property's planning status
4. Do NOT proceed with income projections or offer terms until planning status is confirmed
5. Known London entries: 006 (W4), 052 (SE25)

## Meeting Notes Checklist

After every meeting, capture in Monday.com:

- [ ] Lead name, email, phone
- [ ] Property address and postcode
- [ ] Bedroom count
- [ ] Profile type (STL-SW / EX-STL / PURCH / SELL / ABROAD / R2R)
- [ ] Current situation (LTL, self-managing, empty, purchasing)
- [ ] Timeline / delay type (DLY-TEN / DLY-FUR / DLY-PUR / DLY-MOV / DLY-OTH)
- [ ] Green light signals present (GL-SELF / GL-MGMT / GL-TIME)
- [ ] Emotional archetype identified
- [ ] Qualification score (0-100)
- [ ] Outcome (READY_NOW / READY_SOON / DELAYED / NURTURE / LOST)
- [ ] Specific next action and date
- [ ] Any red flags or sensitivities
