## PURPOSE

This guide covers how to run the web meeting presentation effectively. The lead has already received the Stayful presentation deck before the call. This meeting is not a slide-reading exercise — it is a consultative session where the deck provides structure but the conversation is what closes.

## MEETING STRUCTURE — 45 MINUTES

### PHASE 1 — OPEN AND ORIENT (0–5 min)

**Goal**: Establish rapport, set expectations for the meeting, confirm they've seen the presentation.

**Script**:
"Thanks for making time — good to finally speak properly. You'll have seen the presentation already, so you've got the context. What I want to do today is: understand your situation specifically, walk through the numbers for your property, and answer whatever questions you've got. Sound good?"

**Adapt by profile**:
- **EX-STL**: "You've been with Stayful before — so you know the model. Today is really about what's changed and whether the timing works."
- **PURCH**: "You're still in the purchase process — so we're getting ahead of it, which is smart. Today is about understanding what you'd be coming into once completion happens."
- **SELL**: "You're weighing up selling versus STL — so the numbers need to make a compelling case. Let's see if they do."
- **ABROAD**: "You're planning to relocate — so remote management is the key thing. I want to make sure you understand exactly how that works."

---

### PHASE 2 — PROPERTY DISCOVERY (5–12 min)

**Goal**: Confirm property details, identify readiness, surface any delay signals early.

**Core questions** (ask all, in any order):
- "Tell me about the property — address, bedrooms, current status?"
- "Is it furnished at the moment?"
- "Is there anyone in the property right now — tenant, you, family?"
- "When do you think it could be available for STR?"
- "Have you done any STR before, or would this be the first time?"

**What you're listening for**:
- Unfurnished → flag DLY-FUR, address cost question proactively
- Existing tenant → note lease end date, flag DLY-TEN
- In purchase → confirm completion date, flag DLY-PUR
- Moving abroad → confirm departure date, flag DLY-MOV
- Partner not on call → note, set M2 with both parties

**Do NOT** skip this phase even if you think you know the details. Leads often have updates not reflected in CRM.

---

### PHASE 3 — NUMBERS WALKTHROUGH (12–25 min)

**Goal**: Present the income projection clearly, anchor the lead to their specific numbers, establish value vs alternatives.

**Opening**:
"Let me show you the numbers for your property specifically. I've run the projection based on [address/bedrooms/location]."

**The three-number frame** (always present in this order):
1. **STR Net Income**: "Based on comparable properties in [location], I'd expect you to net around [X] per month after our fee and platform costs."
2. **Long-Term Let Equivalent**: "A long let on the same property would achieve around [LTL] per month. So the STR uplift is [gap]."
3. **Their mortgage/cost**: "Your mortgage is [Y]. So your floor — what you're making even on a quiet month — sits above your costs."

**Handling income uncertainty**:
- If the lead pushes on the estimate: "That's based on comparable properties in [area] over the last 12 months. I can't guarantee it — STR income varies — but that's the realistic range."
- If the lead has seen a higher number elsewhere: "Which tool gave you that? Some estimate gross before platform fees and our fee — the net figure is what matters. Let's look at the same number."
- If the property is in a less common market: "I'll be honest — [location] is [smaller/newer for us]. I'll give you a conservative figure. If it outperforms, that's a bonus."

**Seasonality framing**:
"There are quiet months — December and January especially. The annual average is what you should be measuring against. And that average is consistently above what you'd get on a long let."

---

### PHASE 4 — MANAGEMENT MODEL WALKTHROUGH (25–35 min)

**Goal**: Explain how Stayful operates in enough detail that the lead understands what they're buying. Focus on the points that generate the most positive engagement.

**Must-cover points** (always):
- [ ] Full management — no owner involvement in day-to-day
- [ ] 3-platform + direct booking strategy
- [ ] Individual property listing (not hotel-style grouping)
- [ ] Guest vetting — ID, deposits, no same-night bookings
- [ ] Monthly payout 1st–5th, never missed
- [ ] Monthly statement + Slack channel
- [ ] Maintenance batching, no markups, quarterly invoices
- [ ] 6-month contract, 3-month notice after

**Adapt emphasis by profile**:

| Profile | Emphasise most |
|---------|---------------|
| STL-SW (switching from self-manage) | Time saving, hands-off, direct bookings, guest quality |
| EX-STL (returning) | What's new/improved, communication model |
| PURCH (buying a property) | Onboarding ease, how quickly they go live |
| SELL (considering selling) | Income during sales period, flexibility |
| ABROAD (relocating) | Remote management, quarterly inspections, bank transfer payouts |
| R2R (rent-to-rent) | Full management model, economics at 13%+VAT |

**On the agreement**:
"The agreement is 6 months minimum — after that, 3 months notice either way. We don't lock you in beyond that. The 3-week onboarding covers photography, listing setup, channel manager integration — you don't need to do anything. We handle it."

**On fees**:
"Our standard fee is 15% plus VAT. That's our management fee. Platform fees are separate — Airbnb, Booking.com etc. charge their own fee on top, but that comes off gross before we take ours. The number I've given you is your net take-home."

---

### PHASE 5 — OBJECTION HANDLING (varies)

**Run this phase reactively — don't wait until a formal "objection moment". Handle them as they arise.**

| Objection | Best response |
|-----------|--------------|
| "The fee is too high" | Anchor to net take-home vs long-let. 15% of a bigger number still beats 0% of a smaller one. |
| "I want to try it myself first" | "Most of our best clients tried it first. When you're ready, we'll be here." (Warm park.) |
| "The income estimate seems low" | Ask what they were expecting. Recalibrate to net vs gross methodology. |
| "What about quiet months?" | Acknowledge them. Anchor to annual average, not monthly. |
| "I need to speak to my partner" | "Would it make sense to get [partner] on a quick call this week?" |
| "My tenant's in until [date]" | "That's fine. We can sign now and set activation to [date]." |
| "I'm still waiting for [X]" | Identify the prerequisite. Set a follow-up around it. Deferred offer if timeline is clear. |
| "Your competitors charge less" | "What are they including? Let's compare net take-home." (Never name-compare.) |
| "I've had a bad experience before" | Slow down. Listen fully. Acknowledge the frustration. Then — specifically — explain what's different. |

---

### PHASE 6 — OFFER (35–42 min)

**Only offer if**: No unresolved delay signal, conversion likelihood ≥5, property fits Stayful model.

**Offer framing**:
"I can hold a 13% rate for you — that's below our standard 15% plus VAT. To be clear, that's for the first 12 months, then it reverts to 15%. I'll hold that until [day — typically 48–72 hours]. After that it goes back to standard."

**If they're close but not ready to commit**:
"What I'd suggest is this: take a look at the agreement, come back with any questions. I'll hold the 13% until [day]. After that we revert to standard."

**If there's a delay signal (deferred offer)**:
"I can hold the rate — but I'll tie the activation to [tenant leaving / completion / your departure]. That way you're not on the clock until the delay resolves."

**If they want to think**:
"That's completely fine. The one thing I'd ask is — is there anything that would stop you moving forward once you've had a think? Because if there's a blocker, I'd rather know now so we can address it."

---

### PHASE 7 — CLOSE OR PARK (42–45 min)

**If they're committing**:
"Great. I'll send you the agreement today. Once that's signed, I'll introduce you to the onboarding team and we'll book the photography. You'll be live within 3 weeks."

**If they need a second meeting (M2)**:
"Let's book a 20-minute call for [specific day] — I'll send you a calendar link. By then you'll have had a chance to look at the agreement and we can confirm the start date."

**If they're parking (DELAYED/NURTURE)**:
"That's absolutely fine — [situation] is the right priority. I'll check in with you in [timeframe]. In the meantime, you've got everything you need — the presentation, the numbers. Any questions before then, just message me."

**If it's a LOST**:
"I understand — and I appreciate you taking the time to look at it. If anything changes — whether that's [timing/situation], don't hesitate to come back. The model works when the timing's right."

## COMMON MEETING FLOW VARIATIONS

### Lead is very analytical (asking lots of specific questions)
→ Slow down. Answer each question fully. Do not rush to close. Analytical leads close after they've worked through every question — not before.

### Lead is very enthusiastic but vague
→ Get specific. Ask: "When do you think you'd realistically want to go live?" Enthusiasm without timeline = NURTURE risk.

### Lead reveals a delay signal late in the call
→ Don't try to force through the delay. Acknowledge it. Offer a deferred agreement if timeline is clear. Park with a specific follow-up if not.

### Partner not on the call
→ Always ask: "Is [partner] in a position to look at this too, or is this your decision?" Don't assume — joint owners must both agree.

### Call gets cut short (technical issue / lead has to leave)
→ Immediately book the M2. "Let's not try to rush the rest of this. Can we book 30 minutes this week?" Send the presentation link and key numbers summary by email.

### Lead has clearly already decided against
→ Don't push. Ask: "Is there something that's put you off, or is it just not the right time?" Log the real reason. A pushed-away lead is a burned lead.
