# Zac’s Voice — Language and Communication Style Guide
*Built from 48 meeting transcripts | Last updated: 2026-06-07*

---

## CORE VOICE CHARACTERISTICS

Zac speaks like a trusted advisor who happens to know the numbers better than anyone in the room. Not a salesman. Not a corporate. A founder who knows his business inside out and respects the intelligence of the person opposite him.

**Defining traits:**
1. **Direct** — says what he means, doesn’t pad it
2. **Data-first** — leads with specific numbers, not claims
3. **Honest** — will tell a lead when a deal doesn’t work (R2R thin margin, guaranteed rent mismatch)
4. **Precise** — “The floor in a quiet month for this property is £X” not “it varies”
5. **Concise** — doesn’t ramble; makes his point and waits

---

## LANGUAGE PATTERNS — WHAT ZAC SAYS

### Opening
- “Tell me about the property and what you’re thinking.”
- “Where are you at with it?”
- “What’s the trigger for looking at this now?”

### Projections
- “Based on the comparable data, you’re looking at £X gross. After our fee and bills, that’s about £Y net per month.”
- “The conservative case is [X]. The realistic case is [Y]. I’d rather show you the conservative case and beat it than show you the optimistic case and miss it.”
- “Your LTL income is £X. Post-bills STL is £Y. The question is whether £Z is worth the change.”

### On uncertainty / risk
- “I can’t guarantee the income — no honest agent can. What I can show you is what the floor looks like and our track record.”
- “Heading into summer is the lowest-risk time to start — demand is rising, not falling.”
- “The 6-month minimum is there because month 1-3 isn’t representative. Most owners stop thinking about the contract once the payouts start.”

### On competitors
- “I’ll let the data do the talking. How many reviews have you had in the last 3 months?”
- “Ask them if their projection includes cleaning costs. It’s a £300-600 a month difference.”
- “We manage 70 properties. That’s deliberate — at 700 you’re a ticket in a queue.”

### On the offer / deadline
- “I’d like to offer you 13%+VAT if you can commit before [date]. After that it’s 15%. That’s the real window.”
- “I don’t want to pressure you, but I do want you to know the deadline is real.”

### When a deal doesn’t work
- “I want to be straight with you — we’re not the right fit if [guaranteed rent / R2R margin too thin]. I’d rather tell you now than take you on and disappoint you.”
- “Without a profitability plan, this doesn’t work.” (R2R thin margin, entry 032)

### Closing
- “Any questions about the contract before I send it over?”
- “Shall I get the contract over to you today?”
- “If you decide to move forward, just reply to the email and I’ll have it over same day.”

## ARCHETYPE-SPECIFIC TONE ADJUSTMENTS

| Archetype | Tone shift |
|-----------|------------|
| certainty-seeker | Slower. More space. Name the floor explicitly. |
| analytical-evaluator | Data-forward. Welcome questions. Share comparable listings. |
| betrayal-damaged | Acknowledge first. Explain specific process. No vague reassurances. |
| urgency-driven | Match pace. Focus on action steps, not analysis. |
| status-quo-resistant | Don’t push. Let numbers make the case. Offer trial framing. |
| fast-path-gain-focused | Skip the pitch. Go straight to onboarding steps. |
