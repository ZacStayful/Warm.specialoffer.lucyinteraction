# Stayful Lead Qualification Framework
*Built from 50 meetings | Schema 3.0 | Updated 2026-05-31*

---

## PURPOSE

This framework tells you within the first 10 minutes of a meeting whether a lead is READY_NOW, READY_SOON, DELAYED, or NURTURE. It determines the correct playbook track and whether an offer is appropriate.

---

## TIER 1 — DISQUALIFIERS (Exit immediately if confirmed)

These signals mean Stayful cannot help the lead at all. Do not waste time on a full meeting.

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Property outside Leicestershire / Leeds geography | Apologise, explain coverage area, suggest they research local operators |
| Lead is a renter (doesn't own the property) | Cannot sign a management agreement — check if they have owner permission |
| Property is a commercial unit / HMO / social housing | Not Stayful's model — decline politely |
| Lead has an existing STR manager and zero intention to switch | Park as LOST, no offer |
| Lead says they want to rent long-term only, no STR interest | Redirect to long-let context, no STR pitch |

---

## TIER 2 — QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST

Run through these in the first 8–10 minutes. Each "Yes" increases conversion likelihood.

### Property Basics
- [ ] **They own the property** (not renting, not buying — yet)
- [ ] **Property is in the Stayful geography** (Leicester, Loughborough, Leeds)
- [ ] **Bedrooms: 2+** (1-bed properties perform but are low priority)
- [ ] **Property is furnished or can be furnished** (note: unfurnished = DLY-FUR, not disqualified)
- [ ] **No sitting tenant** (note: existing tenant = DLY-TEN, not disqualified)

### Motivation Clarity
- [ ] **They have a clear financial motivation** (mortgage cover, surplus income, retiring abroad)
- [ ] **They understand STR is NOT passive** (willing to accept occasional involvement)
- [ ] **They've researched STR** or have a baseline expectation to anchor from

### Readiness Signals
- [ ] **Property available within 2 months** — GREEN LIGHT (GL-TIME)
- [ ] **Currently self-managing on Airbnb/Booking** — GREEN LIGHT (GL-SELF)
- [ ] **Currently with a management company** — GREEN LIGHT (GL-MGMT)
- [ ] **No current occupant, property sitting empty** — strong READY_NOW signal

## DELAY SIGNAL IDENTIFICATION

Identify delay signals before making any offer. Delay ≠ lost. Delay = different close strategy.

### DLY-TEN — Existing Tenant
**Triggers**: "I have a tenant in", "they're on a 6-month contract", "tenancy ends in..."
**Questions to ask**:
- When does the tenancy end?
- Are you planning to renew it?
- Would you be open to giving notice now?
**Classification**: READY_SOON if tenancy ends ≤3 months, DELAYED if 3–9 months
**Offer rule**: Do NOT offer during DLY-TEN unless they confirm notice will be served before expiry. Exception: sign agreement now, activation date set to tenancy end.

### DLY-FUR — Unfurnished Property
**Triggers**: "it's not furnished", "I'd need to buy furniture", "what furniture do you need?"
**Questions to ask**:
- Is that something you'd be willing to invest in?
- Have you got a budget for that?
- Do you want a furniture pack recommendation?
**Classification**: READY_SOON (budget confirmed) or NURTURE (budget unclear)
**Offer rule**: Offer only if they confirm willingness to furnish. Note: Stayful doesn't provide furniture; they can recommend suppliers.

### DLY-PUR — Purchasing a Property
**Triggers**: "I'm in the process of buying", "I'm looking at properties", "completion is..."
**Questions to ask**:
- When is completion expected?
- Is the property already selected?
- What's the current stage?
**Classification**: READY_SOON if completion ≤8 weeks, DELAYED if further out, NURTURE if no property identified
**Offer rule**: May offer with agreement activation tied to completion date. High drop-off risk — many purchasing leads fall through.

### DLY-MOV — Moving Abroad
**Triggers**: "we're relocating", "moving to [country]", "going abroad for work"
**Questions to ask**:
- When is the move?
- Is the property your main residence now?
- Will the property be vacant from that date?
**Classification**: READY_SOON if departure ≤8 weeks, NURTURE if vague
**Offer rule**: Exception case — CAN offer even with active delay if departure date is confirmed and imminent. Urgency framing applies.

### DLY-OTH — Other Prerequisite
**Triggers**: "I need to speak to my partner", "waiting for renovation", "need planning permission", "need to sort X first"
**Questions to ask**:
- What needs to happen before you'd be ready?
- When do you think that might be resolved?
- Is there anything Stayful can help with in the meantime?
**Classification**: Case by case. Partner approval = READY_SOON usually. Renovation = DELAYED.
**Offer rule**: Gauge likelihood of the prerequisite resolving. If confident: offer with deferred start. If vague: park as NURTURE.

## GREEN LIGHT SIGNAL IDENTIFICATION

Green lights accelerate the close. Multiple green lights = strong READY_NOW candidate.

### GL-SELF — Currently Self-Managing
**Triggers**: "I manage it myself", "I'm on Airbnb already", "it's taking up too much of my time"
**Why it matters**: They've proven the STR concept works. The only question is whether they want to hand it over.
**Best response**: Acknowledge the effort they've put in. Pivot to time/stress cost. "You've done the hard bit — proving it works. Now let's scale it without the hassle."

### GL-MGMT — Currently With a Management Company
**Triggers**: "I'm with [company]", "my current manager...", "I'm not happy with..."
**Why it matters**: They're already sold on STR management. This is a competitor switch conversation.
**Best response**: Do NOT ask them to criticise the competitor by name. Ask: "What would make switching worth it for you?" Listen before pitching.
**Watch out for**: Betrayal-damaged archetype — had a very bad experience. Needs credibility rebuilt, not just comparison.

### GL-TIME — Ready Within 2 Months
**Triggers**: "it'll be available from [date ≤8 weeks]", "I'm moving out next month", "tenant leaves in 3 weeks"
**Why it matters**: Short time horizon removes most objection surface area.
**Best response**: Anchor to the date. "So if we got the agreement signed this week, we'd have everything ready before [date]. That's ideal."

## OUTCOME CLASSIFICATION

At the end of every meeting, classify the lead:

| Outcome | Definition | Next action |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **READY_NOW** | No delay signals, property available, motivated | Offer immediately, close before call ends |
| **READY_SOON** | Delay signal present but resolvable ≤8 weeks | Conditional offer or agreement with deferred start |
| **DELAYED** | Clear timeline but >8 weeks away | Park with follow-up date, nurture sequence |
| **NURTURE** | Interested but timeline/commitment unclear | Soft follow-up, no offer, re-qualify in 30–60 days |
| **LOST** | Disqualified, dropped out, or confirmed not proceeding | Log reason, close in CRM |

## CONVERSION LIKELIHOOD SCORING

Score each lead 0–10 before the meeting ends.

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Property owned, in geography, furnished/furnishable | +3 |
| Clear financial motivation stated | +1 |
| No delay signal OR delay signal with clear resolution | +2 |
| Green light signal present (any type) | +2 |
| Lead engaged with figures positively | +1 |
| Lead initiated specific next-step questions | +1 |

**Score interpretation**:
- 8–10: Strong READY_NOW. Offer immediately.
- 5–7: READY_SOON. Conditional offer or M2 ask.
- 3–4: DELAYED or NURTURE. No offer; follow-up plan.
- 0–2: Risk of LOST. Diagnose blocker before ending call.

## SPECIAL OFFER DECISION TREE

```
Is there an active, unresolved delay signal?
  YES → Is it DLY-MOV with confirmed departure ≤8 weeks?
    YES → May offer (urgency exception)
    NO  → DO NOT offer. Park as DELAYED/NURTURE.
  NO  → Is conversion likelihood score ≥ 5?
    YES → Is this a competitor switch (GL-MGMT)?
      YES → Check their contract end date. Offer with matching start.
      NO  → Offer standard 13%+VAT special offer.
    NO  → Do NOT offer. Understand the blocker first.
```

Offer expiry: Always set 48–72 hours from call. "I'll hold that until [day]."
