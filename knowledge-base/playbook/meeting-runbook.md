# Stayful Sales Meeting Runbook

Step-by-step guide for running a Stayful web meeting from open to close.
Built from 70-meeting knowledge base.

---

## Pre-Meeting (5 minutes before)

- Pull the lead's entry from Monday.com: name, property address, bedrooms, current situation
- If a previous meeting exists, re-read the call brief
- Check if they are in the knowledge base (existing entry number)
- Note their profile type and any flagged sensitivities
- Have the STR net analyser ready with their postcode entered
- **Check for red flags**: London postcode (90-day rule), R2R arrangement, any existing notes

---

## Opening (0-5 minutes)

**Goal**: Build rapport, establish their baseline, identify the trigger.

1. Warm greeting by name. Reference how they found Stayful if known.
2. Ask: "What made you want to explore short-term letting?"
   - Listen without interrupting. The answer tells you the archetype.
   - Urgency-driven leads give a specific trigger. Status-quo-resistant leads are vague.
3. Ask: "What are you currently doing with the property?"
   - LTL tenant? Note DLY-TEN and check when tenancy ends.
   - Self-managing STR? Profile as STL-SW.
   - With another agent? Profile as EX-STL. Ask who and what's wrong.
   - Empty? Ask why and when it became available.
4. Confirm: name, address, postcode, bedrooms.

---

## Discovery (5-20 minutes)

**Goal**: Build a complete picture of the lead's situation, timeline, and decision-making process.

1. Property details
   - Furnished or unfurnished? (DLY-FUR if unfurnished)
   - Any current occupants or access restrictions?
   - Any work needed before it could be listed?

2. Financial baseline
   - Monthly mortgage or rent?
   - If LTL: what is the current rent received?
   - Pull the STR net analyser while talking

3. Timeline
   - When could they realistically start?
   - Any specific deadlines or pressures?
   - Who else is involved in the decision?

4. History and trust level
   - Have they done STR before? With who? Why did it end?
   - If yes: listen for betrayal-damaged signals
   - If EX-STL: what specifically are they unhappy about?

5. Concerns they have not yet raised
   - "Is there anything about STR that concerns you before I go through the figures?"
   - Listen for unspoken fears

---

## Pitch (20-40 minutes)

**Goal**: Show the income case, explain the model, and answer objections in real time.

1. **Income comparison**
   - Show the STR net analyser output
   - Walk through: gross STR income → minus Stayful 15% → minus platform fee (3%) → minus cleaning → = STR net
   - Compare to: their current LTL rent (or mortgage as the floor)
   - Name the gap: "That's an extra £X per month, or £Y per year."

2. **How Stayful works**
   - Photography, listing creation, pricing optimisation
   - Guest communication: 100% Zac, direct access
   - Maintenance coordination
   - Cleaning management
   - Monthly payout 1st-5th
   - Owner has zero involvement by choice

3. **Protection mechanisms** (adjust emphasis by archetype)
   - Damage: £250 waiver per booking + AirCover
   - Guest vetting: profile review, booking pattern analysis
   - Owner access: block any dates at any time
   - Exit: 3 months notice after initial 6-month term

4. **Social proof** (for social-proof-dependent and betrayal-damaged)
   - 70 properties, direct owner relationships
   - Airbnb Superhost status
   - Monthly payout, never missed

5. **Check in during pitch**: "Does that figure make sense given your current rent?"

---

## Objection Handling

See: `response-library/objections.json` for full scripted responses.

Most common objections in order:
1. 15% is too high → show the income gap, make fee look small
2. Damage concerns → explain the three-layer protection
3. Void periods → show seasonal floor, not just peak
4. Need to think → ask what specifically, don't accept vague stall
5. Partner needs to agree → offer joint call or one-pager to share

---

## Close (final 10 minutes)

**Goal**: Identify conversion readiness and agree on a specific next step.

1. Temperature check: "On a scale of 1 to 10, where are you on this?"
   - 9-10: Send agreement now. Book photographer.
   - 7-8: Identify the specific gap. Fill it before the meeting ends.
   - Below 7: Understand the block. Set a specific follow-up date.

2. Next step commitment:
   - Never end without a specific action and date
   - Options: sign today / call on [date] / documents sent for review / joint call booked

3. Send documents immediately after the meeting:
   - Management agreement
   - STR net analyser output
   - Action plan

---

## Post-Meeting (same day)

1. Update Monday.com:
   - Outcome, next action, date
   - Call brief updated with key notes
   - Archetype and delay/green light signals noted

2. Send follow-up email within 2 hours:
   - Reference what you discussed
   - Include the income figure from the meeting
   - Attach documents if agreed
   - Confirm the next step

3. Diarise the follow-up action in Monday.com

---

## Red Flags — Stop and Check

- **London postcode**: 90-day STR limit. Verify planning before proceeding.
- **R2R arrangement**: Landlord consent required. Do not proceed without it.
- **Entry 016 Ihsan**: Brothel trauma. Extreme sensitivity. Full guest vetting discussion required.
- **SELL profile**: 100% loss rate. Minimal time investment unless numbers are exceptional.
- **R2R profile**: 83% loss rate. Verify landlord involvement immediately.
