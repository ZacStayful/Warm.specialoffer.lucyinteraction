## MEETING STRUCTURE

### 0–5 min: Open and qualify

"Tell me about the property and what you’re thinking."

Listen for:
- Who’s the decision-maker? Is their partner/spouse present?
- What’s the trigger? (Renters Reform / tenant leaving / relocating / bad experience with agent)
- What’s their current income and what do they want to achieve?
- Do they want guaranteed rent? (If yes + 1-bed: pre-qualify out now)

**Don’t:** Start with the pitch. Start with questions.

---

### 5–20 min: Show the numbers

Present projections in this order:
1. Gross annual (headline)
2. Our fee deducted
3. Bills deducted (utilities, council tax, Wi-Fi)
4. **Net monthly figure** — this is what they care about
5. LTL comparison (post-tax, post-maintenance is fairer)

For STL Switch leads: Show 3 scenarios — conservative (65% occ), realistic (70-72%), good (75%+)
For ABROAD leads: Emphasise “no owner involvement needed” alongside the net figure
For EX-STL leads: Show their current agent’s occupancy vs market average first, THEN show income

**Key rule:** Post-bills STL net vs LTL net. Never gross vs gross.

---

### 20–35 min: Handle the real objections

Most common, in order of frequency:
1. "My wife/partner needs to agree" → Book follow-up call now. Don’t close without them.
2. "Can you guarantee it?" → Show floor. Show payout record. Don’t promise a guarantee.
3. "The bills will be my cost" → Show post-bills net comparison.
4. "Another agent quoted more" → Ask if their quote includes cleaning costs.
5. "6 months feels long" → Explain ramp-up rationale.

See `responses/objections.json` for full response scripts.

---

### 35–45 min: The offer

Offer 13%+VAT **only when:**
- No spouse gate (or spouse is present/agreed)
- No unresolved DLY-OTH blocker
- Property is viable (>£12k net)
- At least one GL signal present

**Offer language:** “I’d like to offer you 13%+VAT if you’re able to commit before [date]. After that it’s 15%. That’s the real window.”

**Deadline must be set BEFORE the lead’s gating event:**
- Tenant leaving 30 April → Offer deadline 22 April
- Renovation completing July → Don’t offer yet (defer to post-renovation)
- Spouse not present → Offer in follow-up call, not this meeting

---

### Last 5 min: Close or next step

**If closing:** "Any questions about the contract before I send it? I’ll have it over within the hour."

**If not closing:** Name the specific next step and date. Never end without a concrete next action.
- "Can we get your partner on a call [day] at [time]?"
- "I’ll send the projections over and follow up [day]."
- "Tenant leaves [date] — I’ll call you the week before to confirm."

## GREEN LIGHT SIGNALS — act on these immediately

| Signal | Type | Action |
|--------|------|--------|
| Tenant leaving this month | GL-TIME | Set deadline before tenant leaves |
| Dissatisfied with current agent | GL-MGMT | Show review/performance gap now |
| No mortgage | GL-SELF | Frame as “all income is profit” |
| Relocating abroad | DLY-MOV | Treat relocation date as hard deadline |
| Keys in 2-4 weeks | GL-TIME | Same-day onboarding conversation |

---

## RED FLAGS — pre-qualify out

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Wants guaranteed rent + 1-bed | "We’re not the right fit if guarantee is the requirement." |
| R2R rent > £1,200/mo with <3-bed property | Check margin. If <£2k/yr after fees, decline. |
| Owner not present (company rep only) | Get actual owner on call before any offer |
| Multiple complexity factors (third-party ownership + overseas + refurb needed) | Risk-flag. Unlikely to convert. |
