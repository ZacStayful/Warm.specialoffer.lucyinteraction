# R2R Playbook (Rent-to-Rent)

## Profile Definition
Lead is renting a property from a landlord and intends to sublet it on STL at a higher rate, keeping the margin as profit.

## Critical Pre-Qualification Rule

**Run this check before booking a full meeting.**

### R2R Viability Formula
```
STL net monthly = STL gross × (1 - 0.15)   [assuming 15% fee]
Lead margin = STL net monthly - Rent - Utilities - Furnishing amortisation
```

If `Lead margin < £300/month`, the risk/reward for the R2R lead is questionable. Be honest before they invest time.

### Qualify Out Early
- Rent level leaves near-zero margin (entry 024/025 Rasheed Ajani: £1,000 rent, ~£1,100 STL net = £100 margin before utilities)
- Landlord permission for STL subletting not confirmed — essential legally
- Property is shared accommodation / HMO (Stayful manages whole properties: entry 035)
- R2R deadline has passed (entry 022 Ada Madu: good deal abandoned when signing window closed)

### Qualify In
- STL net monthly significantly exceeds rent
- Lease/landlord explicitly permits STL subletting
- Property is whole-unit (not shared rooms)
- Entry 063 (Vishal Beeharry, CV34 5NX): £399/month margin after rent and bills — tight but viable

## Opening Questions
1. "What rent are you paying the landlord, and is STL permitted in your lease or have you confirmed this with them?"
2. "Have you run the income projections for this postcode at this property size?"
3. "Is the property already furnished or will that be an additional cost?"

## Common Failure Patterns

### Rent Too High (entries 024/025)
Rasheed Ajani, Sheffield S26: £1,000/month rent. STL net ~£1,100. Near-zero margin before utilities.  
**Lesson**: R2R viability is rent-level first, geography second. A great STL market doesn't help if rent is too high.

### Signing Deadline Passed (entry 022)
Ada Madu, Newcastle NE5: viable deal (£14k/yr potential) abandoned because landlord signing window closed.  
**Lesson**: R2R landlord agreements expire. If the lead has a deadline, **prioritise urgency**.

### Thin Margins (entry 031)
Kes Kessington, Manchester M13: rent + management fees consumed STL income.  
**Lesson**: Stayful's 15% fee must be included in the pre-qualification calculation, not treated as optional.

### Shared Accommodation (entry 035)
Mitchell Thomas, Ilkeston DE7: 6-bed shared (HMO-style). Outside Stayful's whole-property model.  
**Lesson**: Clarify in first call. "Do all guests share the property or is each room let separately?" Shared room letting = disqualify.

## Landlord Permission

STL subletting without landlord permission is:
- A breach of tenancy agreement (civil liability)
- Potentially insurance-voiding
- Grounds for eviction

Always confirm landlord permission is secured or being sought before proceeding. Do not onboard an R2R arrangement without confirmation.

## What Works When R2R is Viable

1. **Run the margin calculator live** — showing the numbers builds confidence
2. **Speed** — landlord signing deadlines are real; urgency is legitimate
3. **Furnishing consultation** — R2R leads often need to furnish from scratch; Stayful can advise on what matters for guest scores
4. **Simplicity** — first-time investors (entry 063 Vishal, Touchstone Education background) need a clear process narrative

## The Close (When Viable)

No standard offer applied to R2R — margin must work first. If it does:

```
"The numbers work for you at roughly £X/month after all costs. The next step is 
 getting the management agreement signed — which we can do now — and then 
 confirming your landlord's written permission. Once those two things are in place, 
 we're typically live in 3 weeks. The one time-sensitive element is your landlord's 
 agreement — when does that need to happen?"
```

**Move fast on follow-up for R2R — deadlines are real.**
