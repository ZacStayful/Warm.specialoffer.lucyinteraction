# EX-STL Playbook (EX-STL Switching Manager)

## Profile Definition
Lead currently uses an STL management company and is unhappy with performance. They want to switch to Stayful.

- **Entries**: 10 / 60 (17%)
- **Dominant emotional profile**: loss-averse-switcher (90%), betrayal-damaged (10%)
- **Outcomes**: special_offer_applied (80%), warm (20%)
- **Offer rate**: 80% — highest of any profile type
- **Representative entries**: 015, 036, 038, 039, 043, 045, 046, 047, 058, 060

## When You See This Profile
- Names their current provider voluntarily and with frustration
- Income is below what was originally promised
- Damage reporting failure or maintenance communication breakdown
- Pooled listing identity (their photos used for whole building)
- "I don't want to make the same mistake again"

## Core Rule: Trust Before Income

**Do not pivot to income projections until trust is established.**

For loss-averse-switchers: trust is the primary close. Income is secondary.

**Step 1**: Let them describe what went wrong — fully, without interruption  
**Step 2**: Acknowledge it specifically — do not minimise or defend the industry  
**Step 3**: Show exactly how Stayful handles that exact failure mode  
**Step 4**: Then — and only then — move to income and offer  

---

## Key Objections and Handling

### "They promised £X and I got £Y"
**Profile**: betrayal-damaged (entry 036)  
**Response**: "That gap between what you were told and what arrived is exactly the trust problem. Here's what actually causes income underperformance: review maturity, listing quality, and pricing calibration. Our projections are built from your property's actual comps — not a round number designed to win your business. I'd rather show you what comparable properties actually earned than promise a number I'd have to walk back in January."

### "How are you different from [previous provider]?"
**Do not**: criticise the competitor by name  
**Do**: name their specific failure and explain your exact process for that scenario  

For pooled listing issue (entries 046/047): "Each property gets its own listing, its own photos, its own reviews. You're not competing with 40 other flats — you're running your own brand on Airbnb."  

For damage concealment (entry 045): "We document checkout photographically and report damage within 24 hours. Insurance claims are initiated immediately. That window matters legally — most providers miss it because the check-out process isn't systematic."

For income underperformance (entry 036): "Our pricing algorithm adjusts every 24 hours. Low income in winter is normal across STL — what's not normal is winter income that's lower than it should be because pricing hasn't adapted."

### "Stayful is a relatively new / small company"
**Entry 058** (Martyn De Vere)  
**Response**: "At our size, you get a named person managing your property. If something goes wrong, you message directly and I pick it up. The communication breakdown you described is a scale problem — big companies lose that quality when they grow past a certain point. Our size is why you won't experience that with us."

### "I'm worried about the 6-month commitment"
**Response**: Sign today, 6 months from signature date (not go-live). After initial period: 3 months' rolling notice. Frame it as a structured performance period: "By month 3, you'll know whether we're delivering. If we're not, I want to have that conversation before you have to chase it."

### "I want to sell the property instead" (SELL/EX-STL hybrid)
**Entry 058** (Martyn De Vere, on Rightmove)  
**Response**: STL generates income while the property is on market. 3-month notice after initial term means clean exit when sale completes. Clean exit + income > vacant while waiting for buyer.

---

## Offer Application

Offer is almost always appropriate for EX-STL leads:
- For loss-averse-switchers: 13% reduces the financial downside of trying again
- For betrayal-damaged: offer is the trust bridge — "we're not asking you to take a risk at full price"
- Apply with a specific expiry date tied to their notice period decision point

---

## Follow-up Cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 24–48h | Strike while trust window is open. These leads have often been considering switching for weeks. |
| Day 3 | "Have you had a chance to check the notice period with [current provider]?" |
| Day 7 | If no response, check: is there a contract lock-in? When does it expire? |
| Day 14 | Offer expiry reminder if applicable |

**EX-STL leads don't need nurturing — they need a clean reason to act. Move fast.**
