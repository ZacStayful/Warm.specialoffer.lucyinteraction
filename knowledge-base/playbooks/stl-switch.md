# STL Switch Playbook (STL-SW)

## Profile Definition
Lead owns a property currently on LTL, self-managed, or vacant. They are interested in switching to short-term letting with Stayful as the manager.

## Dataset Summary
- **Entries**: 23 / 60 (38%)
- **Dominant emotional profile**: analytical-evaluator (70%), certainty-seeker (25%)
- **Outcomes**: warm (52%), special_offer_applied (43%), lost (9%)
- **Offer rate**: 43%
- **Representative entries**: 003, 004, 005, 006, 007, 008, 009, 010, 016, 027, 028, 029, 030, 034, 037, 040, 042, 053, 055, 056, 057, 059, 061, 062

## When You See This Profile
- Property has been on LTL for years but owner has seen STL income potential
- Property is vacant (cost push) or soon to become vacant
- Owner has done some income research and wants it validated
- Mortgage may need checking for STL compatibility

---

## Opening Questions
1. "Tell me about the property — how long have you had it, and what's happening with it at the moment?"
2. "Have you done any income research yet, or are we starting fresh today?"
3. "Is there a specific income target you're working towards?"

---

## Income Framing

Lead with **their property's actual Airbnb comparables**, not generic ranges.

- Show month-by-month projection for their location and bedroom count
- Always include worst-month floor (January–March) — **this closes the certainty-seeker**
- Compare to what they're currently earning (LTL net or £0 if vacant)

### For Certainty-Seekers (entries 007, 027, 055, 056, 057, 062)
Lead with the floor first. Never open with peak projections alone.

> "In January — our quietest month — a property like yours nets approximately £X. On a long let, you'd get roughly £Y. So even worst-case, STL still comes out ahead."

### For Analytical-Evaluators (most entries)
Walk through the full cost stack before showing net:
- STL gross revenue (ADR × occupancy × nights)
- Less 15% (or 13%) management fee
- Less utilities estimate
- Less maintenance reserve
- = Net figure they can plan from

## Key Objections and Handling

### "What happens in the quiet months?" / Income consistency concern
**Profile**: certainty-seeker (entries 007, 055, 056, 062)  
**Response**: Show the January floor figure. Even worst month on STL typically exceeds properly-costed LTL net. Payout 1st–5th every month without exception.

### "I want to understand all the costs"
**Profile**: analytical-evaluator  
**Response**: 15% management fee (13% if offer) + utilities (owner pays) + maintenance at cost (no Stayful markup). No onboarding fee, no photography fee, no setup fee from Stayful.

### "I need to talk to my partner/husband/wife"
**Profile**: two-person decision (entry 004 Robert Norris)  
**Response**: Ask what specifically they want to cover together. Offer a one-page summary of key figures. Agree a specific callback date — don't leave it open-ended.

### "My mortgage might not allow STL"
**Response**: This is the lead's responsibility to confirm with their lender. BTL mortgage with STL clause, or consent to let, is required. Many lenders are becoming permissive. Suggest they contact lender with the specific question: "Does my mortgage permit short-term letting platforms such as Airbnb?"

### "I'm looking at other companies too"
**Response**: Good — give them the comparison questions to ask: individual listing vs pooled? Damage reporting SLA? Communication model (call centre vs named person)? Income methodology? See `do-not-say.json` DNS-003.

### "Can you guarantee the income?"
**Profile**: certainty-seeker (entry 027 Liam Yardley — lost on this)  
**Response**: Stayful does not offer guaranteed rent. Show realistic floor instead. If they need a guaranteed rent scheme specifically, be honest this isn't the right product for them. See `objections.json` OBJ-007.

See `closing-scripts.json` → **CLOSE-STL-SW**

Key discipline: keep momentum. Send the management agreement the **same day** as the meeting. Don't let a day pass without the documents in their inbox.

## Offer Decision Framework

Apply 13% offer when:
- Lead is a certainty-seeker profile (locks fee in as permanent security anchor)
- Lead is comparing other providers
- Lead has been warm for 2+ weeks with no decision movement
- A natural deadline exists (departure, tenant exit, seasonal peak approaching)

Do **not** automatically apply for every STL-SW lead. Two converted without offer (entries 034, 044).

## Follow-up Cadence

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 1 | Send management agreement + property action plan + brief meeting summary |
| Day 3–5 | Call or message: "Did you get a chance to look at the documents?" |
| Day 7 | If no response, surface the gate: "Is there one specific thing holding things back?" |
| Day 14 | Final push — mention offer expiry if applicable |
| Day 30+ | Low-touch monthly check-in until decision made |

## Loss Prevention Rules

- **Pre-qualify guaranteed rent seekers** (entry 027): if they need guaranteed rent, disqualify before full meeting
- **Shared accommodation**: Stayful manages whole properties only (entry 035 Mitchell Thomas — lost)
- **Short sale timeline**: if active sale < 3 months and they have an existing STL manager, don't push switch (entry 023)
- **Tax/business rates**: surface this proactively and suggest accountant consultation (entry 028)
- **Contract/performance guarantee seekers**: position as risk-sharing, not guarantees (entry 029)
