# Purchase Playbook (PURCH)

## Profile Definition
Lead is in the process of purchasing a property specifically for STL. They may be pre-completion (off-plan or exchange), awaiting keys, or have just received keys.

## Dataset Summary
- **Entries**: 9 / 60 (15%)
- **Outcomes**: warm (56%), converted (11%), delayed (11%), lost (22%)
- **Offer rate**: 11% — lowest of any profile type
- **Dominant emotional profile**: analytical-evaluator (100%)
- **Representative entries**: 003, 018, 020, 021, 032, 033, 041, 044, 051

---

## The Primary Risk: Developer Management Capture

The most common loss pattern for PURCH leads:
> Developer offers bundled management service at key handover. Overseas or time-pressured buyer defaults to convenience.

**Entry 033** (Mohimi Ovbiagele, off-plan Leicester): lost to developer-appointed management after handover.  
**Prevention**: sign the management agreement **before keys**. By the time the developer is in the room, it's too late.

---

## Opening Questions

1. "When are you expecting to take keys / complete?"
2. "Is the property furnished or will you need to furnish from scratch?"
3. "Has the developer mentioned anything about management services?"
4. "What income target are you working toward, and what does your mortgage look like?"

---

## The Pre-Keys Pitch

```
"The ideal time to sign the management agreement is before you have keys. Here's why: 
we can have photography booked, the listing drafted, and pricing live within 72 hours 
of handover. Your first live date is 2–3 weeks from keys. If you wait until keys to 
start this conversation, you lose those weeks — and in summer, that's £X,000 missed."
```

For developer risk: add "The other thing that happens when you wait: the developer's management company is already in the room at handover. Getting ahead of that conversation now means the decision is already made."

## Income Framing: Year 1 vs Year 2 Trajectory

PURCH leads are buying with a return in mind. They have a target. Show the trajectory.

- **Year 1**: Reviews building, ADR is lower, income is real but not peak. Occupancy builds over first 3 months.
- **Year 2**: 4.8+ rating, direct booking share growing, ~30% uplift vs Year 1 is typical.

**Critical for entry 051** (Don Yoo, 5-bed Coventry): Year 1 net ~£800/month after mortgage. His target is £1,000. The close is Year 2 trajectory + 5-bed vs 4-bed comparable (renovation adding bedroom count).

Don't let Year 1 figures be the only frame of reference. Show the ramp.

## Furnishing / Renovation Delay (DLY-FUR, DLY-PUR)

Many PURCH leads have a gap between commitment and go-live:

**Response**: "Sign the agreement now, and we'll run the setup timeline alongside your renovation. You don't have to wait until the property is ready to start the relationship — in fact, starting now means Stayful can advise on furnishing choices that maximise guest scores."

Booking photography at least 2 weeks before intended live date is essential.

---

## Key Objections and Handling

### "I need to renovate/furnish first"
**Response**: Sign agreement now. Run setup planning in parallel. Stayful can prioritise what matters for guest experience vs what doesn't.

### "My income target is £X — will you hit it?"
**Response**: Show comparable property data. If Year 1 is below target, show Year 2 trajectory and direct booking share growth.

### "The developer said they'd manage it"
**Response**: "A lot of developers offer management as a bundled service — it's convenient, but it's rarely their core competency. The question is: do they specialise in STL? Do they have an active Airbnb presence, a pricing team, and a local operations manager? Stayful's only business is STL. That focus is the difference."

### "I'm not sure STL is right for this property"
**Response**: Run a location demand assessment. Show what comparable properties earn in the same postcode. If it doesn't work, say so — credibility is more valuable than a bad onboarding.

## The Close

See `closing-scripts.json` → **CLOSE-PURCH**

Primary close lever: **speed to income**, not fee level. PURCH leads rarely need an offer to convert — they need a clear path from purchase to first booking.

---

## Offer Framework

Offer is rarely needed (only 1 of 9 PURCH entries received an offer). Apply only when:
- Lead is comparing providers actively
- Off-plan with long gap before completion (entry 032: 4+ month wait)
- Overseas buyer at risk of developer capture

---

## Follow-up Cadence

| Stage | Action |
|-------|--------|
| Pre-keys (4+ weeks out) | Monthly touch — ask for keys date update |
| Pre-keys (2 weeks out) | Book photography slot. Confirm furnishing plan. |
| Keys week | Priority contact. Sign agreement if not yet done. |
| Post-keys | Setup in motion: photos → listing → live |
| Live | First payout 1st–5th of following month |
