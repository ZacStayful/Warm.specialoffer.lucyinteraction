# Offer Triggers Reference

When to make an offer, what rate to offer, and what the constraints are.
Built from the 70-entry knowledge base and conversion analysis.

---

## Offer Rates — Priority Order

### Default: 15%+VAT
Use for all leads unless there is a specific reason not to. Never open with a lower rate.

### Tactical close: 13%+VAT
Available for EX-STL leads or clearly high-intent leads who have stalled after 2+ follow-ups and stated the fee as the specific blocker.

**Conditions to offer 13%**:
- Lead is EX-STL profile (already in managed STR, comparing providers)
- They have explicitly named the 15% as the reason they have not signed
- They have confirmed interest in Stayful in all other respects
- At least 2 follow-up contacts have already happened
- The deadline is specific and real (end of month, photography slot, etc.)

**Conversion evidence**: Entry 019 Taj converted at 13%+VAT with a specific deadline.

### Exceptional: 12%+VAT
**NEVER offer without explicit Zac approval.**
Used once in 70 entries (entry 014 Andrew McIntee). High-value analytical-evaluator who pushed hard on fees. This is a named exception, not a policy.

## Deadline Offer Protocol

A deadline makes the 13% offer significantly more effective. The deadline must be:

1. **Specific**: A named date, not "soon" or "by end of the month"
2. **Tied to something real**: Month end, photography slot, seasonal timing
3. **Clearly explained**: "After [date], the rate returns to our standard 15%"
4. **Followed up**: Contact the day before the deadline expires

**Evidence**: Taj (entry 019) converted partly due to the deadline creating a decision moment after multiple indecisive follow-ups.

## When NOT to Make an Offer

- First or second meeting — too early, removes future lever
- Status-quo-resistant archetype — offer will not overcome inertia
- Betrayal-damaged archetype — price is not the issue, trust is
- Certainty-seeker archetype — a deadline may feel like pressure and cause disengagement
- SELL or R2R profiles — structural issues make offer irrelevant
- R2R without landlord consent — do not negotiate on a structurally disqualified lead

## Green Light Signal Impact on Offer Timing

Offer timing should align with green light signals:

| Green light present | Offer timing |
|---|---|
| GL-TIME | Offer appropriate if fee is the only blocker |
| GL-MGMT | Offer appropriate after establishing Stayful superiority to current agent |
| GL-SELF | Less fee sensitivity typical — try to close at 15% first |
| No green lights | Hold offer. Resolve structural blockers first. |

---

## Fee Objection Handling Before Offer

Before reducing the fee, always attempt the reframe:

1. Show the net income after the 15%
2. Compare to their current baseline
3. Ask: "Is the 15% the specific reason you haven't signed, or is there something else?"
4. Only reduce if 15% is confirmed as the ONLY blocker

If the fee objection is resolved by the reframe — do not reduce. Keep 15%.
