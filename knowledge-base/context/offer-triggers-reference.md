# Offer Triggers — When and How to Make the Special Offer
*Built from 50 meetings | Schema 3.0 | Updated 2026-05-31*

---

## THE STANDARD SPECIAL OFFER

**Rate**: 13% + VAT (vs 15% + VAT standard)  
**Duration**: 12 months from go-live (then reverts to 15% + VAT)  
**Expiry window**: 48–72 hours from call  
**How to set expiry**: "I'll hold that until [specific day]. After that it goes back to 15%."

---

## WHEN TO MAKE THE OFFER

### REQUIRED CONDITIONS (all must be true)

1. **No unresolved delay signal** — exception: DLY-MOV with confirmed departure ≤8 weeks
2. **Property fits Stayful model** — geography, bedrooms, furnishable
3. **Conversion likelihood ≥5** (from qualification scoring)
4. **Lead has engaged with the numbers positively** — not pushing back on income
5. **No major objection left unresolved** — partner sign-off pending = do not offer yet

If any condition is not met: do NOT offer. Park instead.

## THE DECISION TREE

```
Step 1: Check delay signals
  → Active DLY-TEN, DLY-FUR, DLY-PUR, DLY-OTH?
      If YES → Is it DLY-MOV with confirmed departure ≤8 weeks?
        YES → Proceed to Step 2 (urgency exception)
        NO  → DO NOT OFFER. Park with follow-up.
      If NO → Proceed to Step 2.

Step 2: Check property fit
  → In Stayful geography? Furnishable? 2+ beds? Not HMO?
      If NO → DO NOT OFFER. May not be a Stayful fit.
      If YES → Proceed to Step 3.

Step 3: Check engagement signals
  → Lead engaged positively with income figures?
  → Lead asked specific next-step questions?
  → Lead has no unresolved "I need to [X] first" blocker?
      If NO to any → Resolve blocker first. Then reassess.
      If YES to all → OFFER.

Step 4: Check offer type
  → GL-MGMT (competitor switch)?
      → Get contract end date. Match offer activation to contract end.
  → DLY-MOV (confirmed imminent departure)?
      → Deferred activation. "Sign now, starts when you leave."
  → DLY-PUR (near completion)?
      → Deferred activation. "Sign now, starts at completion."
  → Standard READY_NOW?
      → Immediate activation. Standard 48-hour expiry.
```

## HOW TO DELIVER THE OFFER

### Standard framing (READY_NOW)
"Before I let you go, I want to mention one thing. We have a special rate — 13% plus VAT instead of our standard 15%. I can hold that for you until [day — 48–72 hours from now]. After that it reverts. No pressure — if you need more time, just let me know."

### Deferred activation framing (READY_SOON with known date)
"Here's what I'd suggest: we sign the agreement now and tie the start date to [tenant leaving / completion / your departure]. The rate — 13% plus VAT — is locked from now. You're not committed until that date, but everything's ready to go when it arrives."

### Competitor switch framing (GL-MGMT)
"When does your current contract end? [Listen.] OK — we can set the Stayful agreement to kick in from that date. That way there's no overlap and no awkward gap. I'll hold the 13% rate until [day]."

### Urgency framing (DLY-MOV, imminent departure)
"You're going in [X] weeks. You need to sort this before you go — once you're away, the coordination gets harder. If we sign this week, I'll have the photography and listing done before you leave. You'll land in [country] with the property already earning."

## WHAT NOT TO DO WHEN MAKING THE OFFER

| Don't | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Offer when a delay signal is unresolved (except DLY-MOV) | Creates false urgency — lead won't convert |
| Say "I can only hold this for 48 hours" as a pure pressure tactic | If it's not a real limit, don't say it — destroys trust |
| Offer and then immediately reduce the expiry when they hesitate | Signals that the offer isn't real |
| Make the offer before addressing the main unresolved objection | They'll use the delay to avoid answering |
| Repeat the offer multiple times in one call | One clear offer. If they don't respond, ask what the blocker is. |

## IF THE OFFER EXPIRES WITHOUT RESPONSE

**First approach** (24 hours after expiry):
"The 13% rate expired [yesterday/today] — just wanted to let you know we're back to 15%. Happy to honour it if you're still keen, but wanted to be straight with you."

**Second approach** (if they're still warm after expiry):
Generally, do NOT re-offer at 13% immediately. Either:
- Park at standard 15% rate, acknowledge the expiry honestly
- If strategic relationship: could offer once more with a "final" framing and a hard date

**What the data shows**: Leads who let offers expire without response have very low re-engagement rates. Only follow up once on an expired offer, then park.

## MEETING 2 (M2) — THE SECOND CLOSE OPPORTUNITY

When the lead doesn't close on Meeting 1, the M2 is the next close window.

**When to ask for M2**:
- Lead said "I need to look at the agreement" — book M2 explicitly
- Partner needs to be involved — book M2 with both parties
- Delay signal present but resolving — schedule M2 around resolution
- Call ended early for any reason — book M2 immediately

**M2 ask framing**:
"Let's get 20 minutes booked for [specific day]. By then you'll have had a chance to look at the agreement and I can answer any questions — then we'll confirm the start date."

**At M2**:
- Ask: "Have you had a chance to look at the agreement?"
- Address any remaining questions
- Re-state the offer (or current rate if offer expired)
- Close: "So, shall we confirm the start date?"

**M2 close rate**: Not formally tracked in dataset, but entries with a confirmed M2 ask show higher conversion rate than entries without.

## OFFER BY PROFILE TYPE — CALIBRATION

| Profile | Offer timing | Special considerations |
|---------|-------------|----------------------|
| STL-SW | After numbers resonate, before end of call | Time value pitch — "stop managing it yourself" |
| EX-STL | Early — they already know the model | May waive 13% and offer other flexibility |
| PURCH | Deferred — tied to completion | Don't offer before exchange confirmed |
| SELL | Only if income pitch wins vs capital gain | Rarely converts — be honest about the comparison |
| ABROAD | Exception rule — urgency framing | Must have departure date confirmed |
| R2R | Assess economics at 13%+VAT | Some R2R models don't work at this rate |
