# Emotional Profile Summary — Field Reference
*Built from 48 meeting transcripts | Last updated: 2026-06-07*

Use this in pre-call prep to identify the lead’s archetype and calibrate approach before the meeting.

---

## HOW TO IDENTIFY THE ARCHETYPE IN 2 MINUTES

Ask: “What’s the main thing you’re hoping to get from making this change?”

| Answer type | Archetype |
|-------------|----------|
| “Consistent, reliable income” | certainty-seeker |
| “I’ve had a terrible experience and I need someone I can trust” | betrayal-damaged |
| “I just want to see the numbers” | analytical-evaluator |
| “I need to sort this before we leave for [country]” | urgency-driven |
| “Not sure it’s worth the change” | status-quo-resistant |
| “I’ve heard STL is good, I want to do it” | fast-path-gain-focused |
| “I’m worried about what happens if Renters Reform…” | loss-averse-switcher |

---

## CERTAINTY-SEEKER
**Fear:** Variable income. “What if it’s a bad month?”
**Need:** A floor they can bank on.
**Don’t:** Promise a guarantee or dismiss variability concerns.
**Do:** Show monthly floor. Emphasise payout reliability. Frame range as “upside with a floor”.
**Hard stop:** Guaranteed rent on 1-bed = pre-qualify out.

---

## BETRAYAL-DAMAGED
**Fear:** Being let down again. Trusting another agent.
**Need:** Proof of specific processes, not vague reassurances.
**Don’t:** Say “we’re better than them” or “don’t worry”.
**Do:** Name the specific thing that went wrong and explain Stayful’s specific process for it.
- Bad reviews? “Here’s how we generate reviews.”
- Maintenance failure? “Here’s our maintenance response process.”
- Hotel-style listing? “Every property gets its own identity, not a block listing.”
- Tenant misuse? “Here’s how we vet guests.”

---

## ANALYTICAL-EVALUATOR
**Fear:** Making a wrong financial decision.
**Need:** Clean data, honest comparisons.
**Don’t:** Oversell. Use vague language. Avoid hard questions.
**Do:** Lead with projections. Show conservative + realistic. Welcome their questions. Use case studies.
**Danger:** If competitor has better-looking (but dishonest) numbers, name the specific gap (cleaning costs).

---

## URGENCY-DRIVEN
**Fear:** Running out of time before solving a real problem.
**Need:** A clear action plan for the next 2-3 weeks.
**Don’t:** Get stuck in analysis. Slow them down.
**Do:** Match their pace. Close on the deadline. Book the photographer before the call ends.

---

## STATUS-QUO-RESISTANT
**Fear:** Disruption. Change for its own sake.
**Need:** Strong enough reason to move. Minimal friction promise.
**Don’t:** Push. High-pressure tactics backfire.
**Do:** Let projections make the case. Offer trial framing. Explain how Stayful handles the hard parts.

---

## LOSS-AVERSE-SWITCHER
**Fear:** Losing tenants / losing income / regulatory risk.
**Need:** STL framed as protection, not change.
**Don’t:** Frame STL as an exciting opportunity (they’re not excited, they’re anxious).
**Do:** Lead with Renters Reform threat. Frame STL as protection from tenant legislation. Better comms than existing agent.

---

## FAST-PATH-GAIN-FOCUSED
**Fear:** Wasting time on analysis.
**Need:** Clear next steps. Onboarding conversation.
**Don’t:** Re-do projections they already accept. Over-explain.
**Do:** Skip the pitch. Book photography. Order key safe. Build listing.
