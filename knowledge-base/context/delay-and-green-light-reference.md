# Delay Signal & Green Light Signal — Quick Reference
*Built from 50 meetings | Schema 3.0 | Updated 2026-05-31*

## DELAY SIGNAL CODES

### DLY-TEN — Existing Tenant
**Count in dataset**: 3  
**What it means**: Property currently occupied by a tenant under a rental agreement  
**Typical timeline to resolution**: 1–9 months (depends on tenancy length)  
**Outcome distribution**: Usually DELAYED or READY_SOON  

**Identification triggers**:
- "I have a tenant in"
- "They're on a 6-month AST"
- "The tenancy ends in [month]"
- "I need to give notice first"

**Qualifying questions**:
- When does the tenancy end?
- Are you planning to renew it?
- Would you consider serving notice now?
- Is it an AST (periodic, break clause)?

**Offer decision**:
- DO NOT offer if tenant end date is >8 weeks and notice hasn't been served
- CAN offer with deferred activation if tenant end date is confirmed and within 3 months
- Exception: if lead confirms they will serve notice before end of call

**Conversation approach**:
"We can sign the agreement now and tie the activation date to when your tenant leaves — you're not committed until then, but everything's set up and ready to go."

### DLY-FUR — Unfurnished Property
**Count in dataset**: 2  
**What it means**: Property lacks furniture required for STR (bed, sofa, dining table, white goods as minimum)  
**Typical timeline to resolution**: 2–8 weeks (if budget available)  
**Outcome distribution**: READY_SOON (if budget confirmed), NURTURE (if budget unclear)  

**Identification triggers**:
- "It's not furnished"
- "I'd need to buy furniture"
- "What furniture do you need?"
- "It's just been refurbished but there's nothing in it"

**Qualifying questions**:
- Is furnishing something you'd be willing to invest in?
- Have you got a budget for that?
- Do you want a recommendation on furniture packages?
- What's currently in the property?

**Offer decision**:
- DO NOT offer if lead hasn't confirmed willingness to furnish
- CAN offer once they confirm budget and intent to furnish
- Note: Stayful does not supply furniture — recommend suppliers only

**Conversation approach**:
"Furnishing is a one-off cost — typically £3,000–6,000 depending on spec. Once that's done, it's done. The uplift in STR income vs long-let pays that back quickly. Is that something you'd be willing to sort?"

---

### DLY-PUR — Purchasing a Property
**Count in dataset**: 7 (highest frequency delay type)  
**What it means**: Lead doesn't yet own the property — in the process of buying it  
**Typical timeline to resolution**: 2–16 weeks (highly variable)  
**Outcome distribution**: HIGH DROP-OFF RISK — many purchasing leads never complete  

**Identification triggers**:
- "I'm in the process of buying"
- "I'm looking at properties"
- "Completion is in [month]"
- "I'm waiting on the mortgage offer"
- "We've exchanged but not completed"

**Qualifying questions**:
- When is completion expected?
- Is the property already identified/secured?
- Have you exchanged contracts?
- What's the current stage in the purchase process?

**Offer decision**:
- CAN offer with activation tied to completion date if exchange has happened
- DO NOT offer if no property identified — too high a drop-off risk
- Set a follow-up for post-completion, not pre-exchange

**Conversation approach**:
"We can get everything lined up now so you're ready to go the day you get the keys. We'd set the agreement start to your completion date."

**Risk note**: 7 DLY-PUR leads in dataset — only 1 confirmed conversion (Gareth Howard/044). High volume but low yield. Prioritise leads with exchange confirmed.

### DLY-MOV — Moving Abroad
**Count in dataset**: 5  
**What it means**: Lead is relocating internationally and the property will become vacant  
**Typical timeline to resolution**: 2–16 weeks  
**Outcome distribution**: READY_SOON if departure is soon; NURTURE if vague  

**Identification triggers**:
- "We're relocating to [country]"
- "Moving abroad for work"
- "Going overseas"
- "We're going to [country] in [month]"

**Qualifying questions**:
- When is the move?
- Is the property your primary residence now?
- Will the property be vacant from that date?
- Do you have a confirmed departure date?

**Offer decision**:
- **EXCEPTION CASE**: CAN offer even with active delay if departure is confirmed and within 8 weeks
- Urgency framing applies — they need to sort this before they leave
- Deferred activation works well: "Sign now, we're ready when you go"

**Conversation approach**:
"This is actually the ideal scenario for us — you're going, the property sits empty otherwise, and we manage it entirely remotely. You'd never need to come back for it. The income lands in your account 1st to 5th every month."

### DLY-OTH — Other Prerequisite
**Count in dataset**: 4  
**What it means**: A specific blocker that doesn't fit another delay type — partner approval, renovation, planning, etc.  
**Typical timeline to resolution**: Variable  
**Outcome distribution**: Case by case  

**Common sub-types**:
- **Partner not on call** — most common DLY-OTH; resolve by getting partner on M2
- **Renovation in progress** — confirm timeline; offer deferred if <8 weeks
- **Planning permission** — longer tail; park as DELAYED
- **Mortgage/finance question** — ask them to consult broker; offer once resolved
- **Another property first** — clarify sequence; set follow-up

**Qualifying questions**:
- What needs to happen before you'd be ready?
- When do you think that might be resolved?
- Is the decision yours alone, or does someone else need to be involved?

**Offer decision**:
- Depends on specifics — assess probability of resolution
- Partner approval: offer with understanding that partner must confirm on M2
- Renovation <8 weeks: deferred offer
- Renovation >8 weeks: park as DELAYED, follow up around resolution date

## GREEN LIGHT SIGNAL CODES

### GL-SELF — Currently Self-Managing
**Count in dataset**: ~8 (estimated from language signals)  
**What it means**: Lead is already doing STR independently — Airbnb, Booking.com, etc.  
**Why it matters**: They've proven the concept. The conversation is about handing it over, not convincing them STR works.  

**Identification triggers**:
- "I manage it myself on Airbnb"
- "It's taking up so much of my time"
- "I've been doing it for [X] months/years"
- "I'm getting bookings but it's a lot of work"
- "I handle everything myself at the moment"

**Best response approach**:
"You've done the hard work — proving it works, building the listing, getting reviews. The question now is whether you want to keep doing all of that yourself, or hand it to someone who does it all day. The income difference between self-managing and managed is usually smaller than people expect."

**Close angle**: Time value and stress cost. Not income uplift (they're already earning). The pitch is freedom.

---

### GL-MGMT — Currently With a Management Company
**Count in dataset**: ~5 (estimated)  
**What it means**: Lead is using a competitor STR management company and wants to switch  
**Why it matters**: They're already sold on STR management as a concept. This is a competitor switch conversation.  

**Identification triggers**:
- "I'm with [Pass the Keys / MyProperty Host / Sykes / other]"
- "My current manager..."
- "I'm not happy with..."
- "I'm looking at alternatives"
- "My contract is up in [month]"

**Best response approach**:
"I won't ask you to criticise who you're with — that's not useful. What I'd rather ask is: what would make switching worth it for you? What's missing that you'd want to see?"

**Watch for**: Betrayal-damaged archetype — had a genuinely bad experience (damage not reported, hotel-style listing, poor communication). These leads need credibility rebuilt through specifics, not platitudes.

**Common switch triggers from dataset**:
- Hotel-style listing (reviews shared across properties)
- Poor communication / slow response
- Maintenance not handled or marked up
- Platform fees passed through with markup
- Low direct booking ratio
- Income less than expected

**Key differentiators to emphasise**:
- Individual listing (their reviews are theirs)
- Slack channel + monthly reporting
- Maintenance batching, no markups
- 40% direct booking rate
- Payout 1st–5th, never missed

---

### GL-TIME — Available Within 2 Months
**Count in dataset**: ~12 (estimated)  
**What it means**: Property will be available for STR within 8 weeks  
**Why it matters**: Short time horizon removes most delay-related objection surface. Close is now.  

**Identification triggers**:
- "It'll be available from [date within 8 weeks]"
- "I'm moving out next month"
- "The tenant leaves in [X] weeks"
- "We can be ready by [date]"

**Best response approach**:
"Perfect — if we get the agreement signed this week, we'd have everything ready before [date]. The photography and listing setup takes 3 weeks, so we'd be live right on time."

**Close angle**: Anchor to the timeline. Make them see the signed agreement as the logical next step to hit the date.

## SIGNAL COMBINATIONS AND THEIR MEANING

| Combination | Interpretation | Action |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| GL-SELF + GL-TIME | Strong READY_NOW | Offer immediately. Close before call ends. |
| GL-MGMT + GL-TIME | Strong READY_NOW competitor switch | Get contract end date. Offer with matching start. |
| DLY-PUR + GL-TIME (close completion) | Conditional READY_SOON | Deferred offer tied to completion. |
| DLY-TEN + GL-MGMT | Competitor switch with tenant delay | Note tenancy end. Deferred offer to match. |
| DLY-MOV + GL-TIME (confirmed departure) | Exception offer applies | Urgency framing — they need to sort before they go. |
| DLY-OTH (partner) + GL-SELF | READY_SOON pending partner | Get M2 with partner. Offer conditional. |
| Multiple delays + no green lights | DELAYED or NURTURE | Do not offer. Park. |
| SELL profile + no green lights | High LOST risk | Income pitch must clearly win vs capital gain. Rarely does. |
